Relationship between Gini Coefficient, Unemployment rate and GDP

Gini coefficient is a measure of income inequality, ranging from 0 to 1. 0 specifies perfect equality, where everyone has the same income, while 1 specifies perfect inequality, where one has all the income and others have none.

High or low Gini coefficients can have low unemployment rate. Most countries with a Gini coefficient of 0.4 to 0.7 have an unemployment rate of less than 10%.

Most countries with an unemployment rate of over 10% have a low GDP of less than USD 50B.

Africa has most of the countries in the lowest GDP brackets of under USD 10B or 50B, while most European countries have a GDP of at least USD 100 Billion.
